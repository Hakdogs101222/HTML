Green Day

About the Band

Green Day is an American rock band formed in 1987, known for their energetic punk sound and their rebellious presence in the punk rock scene.

They rose to global fame with their album "Dookie," which delivered raw emotion, fast-paced riffs, and unforgettable anthems that helped define a generation.

Beyond their music, Green Day has inspired countless fans with themes of individuality, angst, social commentary, and the chaotic fun of youth—shit n stuff.

Popular Songs

Some of Green Day's most iconic tracks include "Basket Case," "Holiday," and "Boulevard of Broken Dreams," each showcasing the band's signature mix of punk energy and emotional depth.

Band Members

Green Day is composed of Billie Joe Armstrong (vocals, guitar), Mike Dirnt (bass), and Tré Cool (drums). Their chemistry, personality, and chaotic energy on stage have helped shape their iconic punk identity.

Impact on Music

The band revolutionized pop‑punk, bringing the genre into the mainstream with their bold themes, explosive performances, and unforgettable melodies. Their music continues to influence new artists and resonate with fans worldwide.

Legacy

From underground punk roots to global acclaim, Green Day remains one of the most defining rock bands of the modern era. Their albums, attitude, and storytelling have carved a legacy that still thrives today—labas lahat, walang atras, tunay na punk ang dating.
